The fova promise

Free by default.
No account needed.

Every feature works without a paid account. Local LLMs — Ollama, vLLM, LM Studio — run out of the box, and the whole free knowledge stack is no-auth. Paid LLMs and wet-lab submission are the only opt-ins, and they stay optional.

The loop

From a sentence to the bench.

Describe a target in plain language. fova does the rest — and tracks every design back to the intent that produced it.

⌖
01 ▸ STEP

Plans

Turns a natural-language target into a grounded DesignPlan, backed by current literature from Europe PMC, OpenAlex, Semantic Scholar & bioRxiv.

⠿
02 ▸ STEP

Orchestrates

Runs experimentally validated tools — BindCraft, RFdiffusion, ProteinMPNN, AlphaFold3 — locally or on your own Modal GPUs.

≋
03 ▸ STEP

Ranks

Scores every design by ipSAE — the modern interface metric — then filters to a small, high-confidence shortlist.

✓
04 ▸ STEP

Ships

Sends the survivors to the bench through the Adaptyv Foundry API — then closes the design–test–learn loop with real results.

Why fova

Principles, not features.

fova is built on eight design principles. These six are the ones you feel every time you run it.

⊹

Free by default

Local LLMs work out of the box. No account, no key, no card. Paid LLMs and wet-lab submission are opt-in.

✓

Validated tools only

Every built-in design tool has documented wet-lab success. No unproven methods ship in the box.

≋

Ranked by ipSAE

The interface metric that outperformed ipTM across 3,766 tested binders on 15 targets.

⌗

Provenance everywhere

Every design carries full lineage — intent → tool versions → wet-lab result — in local SQLite.

◇

Local-first, cloud-elastic

Runs offline with Ollama. Scales out to your own Modal account for GPU when you need it.

▸

Human-in-the-loop

A confirmation checkpoint before anything slow, costly, or irreversible — including every wet-lab submission.

Orchestrated tools

Only tools with wet-lab receipts.

fova drives state-of-the-art design, structure, and knowledge tools — and shows the LLM type-safe outputs, never raw stdout.
